## Decision: KILL

The illiquid H10 8-K signal **does not survive realistic small-capital costs** at any order size
or any reasonable cost assumption.

---

## The hard numbers

**Median illiquid name ADV: $35,822.** A $5,000 order is 14% of the stock's entire daily volume.

**Round-trip cost at $5K/name (k=10 baseline): 813 bps.** This is not a rounding error.  
The OOS gross alpha in the illiquid cohort at 1d is roughly +1–3% (pre-cost). The cost is 8× larger.

**Under the most optimistic impact assumption (k=5), $5K/name:**
- 1d OOS net: t=+0.15 (noise)
- 3d OOS net: t=+1.42 (below the pre-registered t≥2 bar)
- Does not survive the 2× spread stress test at 1d (goes negative)

**Capacity ceiling: $0** at any practical order size. The 1% ADV participation constraint limits each
name to a maximum of $358/position (1% of $35,822 median ADV). No practical position size exists.

---

## Why "small-capital" doesn't save these names

The SMALL-CAPITAL ADVANTAGE — paying less per trade because $5K is tiny relative to daily volume — only
applies when $5K IS actually tiny. In liquid names (ADV $66M), $5K is 0.0075% of ADV and costs 33 bps
round-trip. In the illiquid names where the alpha lives (ADV $36K), $5K is 14% of ADV and costs 800+ bps.

The pre-registration correctly anticipated both possibilities. The hypothesis was "at Ben's scale you're
a tiny fraction of even an illiquid name's volume." This is wrong for the bottom-ADV tertile. At $35K
median ADV, a $5K position is enormous. The structural-alpha-for-small-capital argument applies only when
absolute order size in dollars is small relative to the stock's liquidity — which requires the stock to
have enough liquidity for the order to not dominate its own daily flow.

---

## The honest read on what the data shows

There IS a real pre-cost signal in the illiquid 8-K cohort:
- OOS (pre-cost, open entry) at 1d: ~+1.7%, at 3d: ~+3.1% — consistent with the pooled H10b OOS result
- This is genuine drift: small-cap stocks react to 8-K filings over 1–3 days

The signal is real. The stocks are just too illiquid to extract any of it after transaction costs.
At the required position sizes, you ARE the market for these names.

---

## Disposition

- **H10 8-K signal (illiquid tertile):** DEAD at all tested order sizes and cost assumptions. No viable
  execution path at Ben's $5–10K position scale.
- **H10 8-K signal (liquid tertile):** Previously confirmed DEAD (Gate 3 in H10b, t≈0.5 OOS).
- **H10 overall verdict:** KILL. The alpha is genuine but wholly inaccessible.

## Implication for research direction

Commit fully to the **LIQUID hunt** (H4/H5 and successors). The small-capital-in-illiquid-names framing

The liquid names (ADV $66M+) have manageable costs (~33 bps rt at $5K) but no detectable 8-K alpha.
Any future candidate signal must have enough alpha in names liquid enough to trade.

There is no AMBIGUOUS outcome here. The illiquid signals are KILL at Ben's real scale.
